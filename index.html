More About Us

Who Are We.

The Centre for Journalism Innovation and Development (CJID) is a non-governmental organization, founded in 2014, to promote a truly independent media landscape that advances fundamental human rights, good governance and accountability in West Africa through investigative journalism, open data and civic technology.

Our Vision

A truly independent media landscape that advances fundamental human rights, good governance and accountability in West Africa.

Our Mission

To deploy investigative journalism, open data and civic technology tools for the advancement of fundamental human rights, good governance and accountability in West Africa.

Advocacy

To promote press freedom, freedom of expression, net neutrality and media plurality.

Capacity Building

To train journalists, civil society and government institutions on various components of development communication.

Civic Technology

To develop civic tools to track, monitor, report and present information useful for decision making by governments and citizens.

Investigative Journalism

To conduct fact-based and data-driven investigations to hold government and institutions to account at all times.

Open Data

To deploy data analysis and its tools to increase access to public and hidden data within government institutions.
